Our Impact & Projects

Headline: Evidence of Change Across Africa

Our work translates into measurable change on the ground. We are proud of the partnerships we have built and the results we have achieved together. Below are some of the key initiatives our team members have spearheaded, demonstrating our commitment to evidence-based, community-centered solutions.

Governing Climate Mobility Project: This project involved extensive participatory fieldwork in Ghana’s Yilo Krobo and Wa West districts to explore gendered adaptation and climate migration. Our work facilitated workshops that empowered women to articulate their specific climate risks and priorities and produced policy briefs for NGOs and policymakers.

Read More

Adaptation at Scale in Semi-arid Regions (ASSAR) Project: As part of this large-scale initiative, our team was instrumental in coordinating field activities for researchers and leading the organization of local, national, and international workshops. The project focused on developing research outreach events to ensure findings reached communities and decision-makers

Read More

Digital Tools for Agriculture and Livelihoods (DigiTAL) Project: Our technical experts designed and implemented communication tools for local research dissemination. A key success was the production of a local Agriculture and Livelihood Radio talk show , alongside blogs and videos, directly connecting researchers with practitioners on the ground.

Read More

CLARE Programme - Migration Research & Media Collaborative: Our thematic experts lead migration-focused research projects under this program. The work focuses on bridging academic research and media outputs to enhance policy relevance and public engagement on climate mobility

Read More

Climate and Development Knowledge Network (CDKN) Country Engagement: In Ghana, we spearheaded the design and execution of activities to promote gender-responsive and socially inclusive climate-resilient development. A major focus is identifying opportunities to integrate gender equality into national and local policies and facilitating access to equitable climate finance.

Read More

Strengthening Northern Borders Project: In partnership with the University of Ghana and IOM, our researchers conducted critical research in border communities. This involved perception surveys and focus group discussions that specifically highlighted women's security and adaptation concerns , leading to briefs for local authorities on inclusive climate and security governance.

Read More

Africa-EU Innovation Alliance for Water and Climate (AfriAlliance): As Project Advisors, our team led proposal writing activities and designed the communication strategy for the "Planning for Drought (P4D)" project. We played a key role in stakeholder engagement and the facilitation of workshops at the local level.

Read More

FAO Indigenous Adaptation Project: As Regional Consultants for the FAO, our experts conducted field visits across West and East Africa to document indigenous Climate Change Adaptation mechanisms. This work involved reviewing the scientific validity of local coping strategies and presenting the findings at the regional headquarters in Ghana.
