About Us

The SAG Initiatives Story: Forging a Resilient Future for Africa

Strategic African Governance (SAG) Initiatives was founded on a simple yet powerful premise: that effective governance is the cornerstone of sustainable development and climate resilience in Africa. We are a consultancy of experts, practitioners, and visionaries deeply committed to driving sustainable, inclusive, and evidence-based change across the continent

With a dedicated focus on climate resilience, governance, and policy engagement, we support governments, organizations, and communities to design and implement impactful solutions that are built to last

  • Our Mission To empower African institutions, leaders, and communities to build climate-smart policies and accountable governance systems that foster sustainable development
  • Our Vision An Africa where resilient communities, responsive institutions, and forward-looking policies shape a just and sustainable future.

Our Core Values

Our Team: Experts with Deep African Roots

At SAG Initiatives, our strength lies in our people. Our team comprises accomplished researchers, policy advisors, and development practitioners with extensive, field-based experience across Africa. We combine rigorous academic expertise with a deep understanding of local political and social dynamics.

Dr. Gatsey is an accomplished researcher and policy analyst who provides strategic leadership for SAG's governance-focused research and advocacy initiatives. With a PhD in Geography and Resource Development, her work specializes in the intersection of climate change, migration, gendered adaptation, and social equity. She brings strong expertise in coordinating multi-stakeholder research projects, engaging in high-level policy dialogues, and using community-centered advocacy to drive change. Her skills include gender analysis, participatory research methods, and translating complex evidence into accessible knowledge products like policy briefs and documentaries.

Dr. Prince Ansah is a climate and development expert with a PhD in Environmental and Geographical Science. He excels in designing and executing in-country strategies that promote gender-responsive and socially inclusive climate-resilient development. His work focuses on facilitating access to equitable climate finance , mainstreaming climate issues from national to local levels , and using innovative communication tools—from local radio shows to web platforms—to bridge the gap between researchers and communities. He has a proven track record in project management, stakeholder engagement, and proposal writing for major international partners.
